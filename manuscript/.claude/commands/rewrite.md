Rewrite the specified section(s) of the manuscript for stronger academic quality. Target section: $ARGUMENTS

## Before rewriting
1. Read the current manuscript.
2. Identify the section's current job and weaknesses.
3. Check that the evidence picture is stable — do not make prose more assertive than the evidence supports.

## Rewrite priorities
- Sharper novelty positioning
- Clearer contributions
- Tighter motivation
- Better related-work differentiation against closest prior work
- Scoped claims over broad marketing language
- Replace vague novelty statements with exact differentiators
- Never claim significance without statistical or methodological support

## Section-specific guidance

**Abstract**: Must state problem, method, concrete evidence/result, and scoped takeaway. No all-motivation abstracts.

**Introduction**: Must state problem/gap, why existing work is insufficient, what the paper contributes, why evidence matters. Contributions should not be buried or repeated inconsistently.

**Related Work**: Organize by axes that matter to the paper. Discuss closest threatening baselines directly. Explicit differentiation, not a literature laundry list.

**Methods**: State assumptions, design choices, and objective clearly. Enough detail to understand what changed vs prior work. Novelty must be isolable.

**Results/Discussion**: Each table/figure answers a question. Interpret quantitative results, don't just restate. Include failure cases and caveats where they matter.

**Conclusion/Limitations**: Close the argument, don't just repeat. Limitations must be concrete and decision-relevant. No broader claims than the body supports.

## Output
Provide the rewritten section text, then a brief note on what changed and why.
