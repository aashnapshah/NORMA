Plan and draft a reviewer rebuttal. Input: $ARGUMENTS

## Steps

1. Read the reviewer comments (from the provided file, text, or arguments).
2. Convert reviews into an issue matrix.
3. Group issues into: fix in paper, answer with existing evidence, answer with new analysis, or concede as limitation.
4. Prioritize points that could change the decision.
5. Answer the most decision-relevant reviewer points first.
6. Pair each response with either manuscript edits, new evidence, or an explicit limitation.

## Output format

```
# Rebuttal Plan

## Global Strategy
[2-4 sentences on what to fix in the paper, what to answer with existing evidence, and what to concede.]

## Rebuttal Matrix
| Reviewer | Issue | Category | Response Strategy | Evidence Needed | Manuscript Change |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

```

## Guidelines
- Be honest — concede valid weaknesses as limitations rather than dismissing them.
- Provide evidence for rebuttals, not just assertions.
- Prioritize by acceptance impact, not by convenience.
